SECTION 1: CS CORE COURSES — LOWER DIVISION
================================================================================

CS 1301 — Introduction to Computing
Credit Hours: 3
Prerequisites: None
Offered: Fall, Spring, Summer

Introduction to fundamental computing principles including problem solving,
algorithm design, and programming. Students learn to write, read, execute, and
debug code using Python. Topics include variables, loops, conditionals, functions,
recursion, data structures (lists, dictionaries), file I/O, and basic object-oriented
programming concepts. This is the gateway course for all CS, Computer Engineering,
and related majors at Georgia Tech.
Minimum grade to satisfy prerequisite: C or better.

Courses unlocked by CS 1301:
  - CS 1331 (Introduction to Object-Oriented Programming)
  - CS 4400 (Introduction to Database Systems)
  - ECE 2020 (Digital System Design)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

CS 1331 — Introduction to Object-Oriented Programming
Credit Hours: 3
Prerequisites: CS 1301 (grade of C or better)
Offered: Fall, Spring, Summer

Introduction to techniques and methods of object-oriented programming including
encapsulation, inheritance, and polymorphism. Emphasis on software development
and individual programming skills. Students program in Java. Topics include
classes and objects, interfaces, abstract classes, exception handling, file I/O,
recursion, generics, and introductory data structures (linked lists, stacks,
queues, trees). Serves as a core prerequisite for CS 1332, CS 2110, and CS 2340.
Minimum grade to satisfy prerequisite: C or better.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

CS 1332 — Data Structures and Algorithms for Applications
Credit Hours: 3
Prerequisites: CS 1331 (grade of C or better)
Offered: Fall, Spring, Summer

Computer data structures and algorithms in the context of object-oriented
programming. Focus on software development towards applications. Topics include:
arrays, linked lists, stacks, queues, deques, trees (BST, AVL, 2-4, heaps),
hash maps, graphs, sorting algorithms (bubble, insertion, selection, merge,
quick, radix), pattern matching algorithms, and graph traversals (BFS, DFS,
Dijkstra's, MST). Algorithm analysis using Big-O notation.
Minimum grade to satisfy prerequisite: C or better.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

CS 2050 — Introduction to Discrete Mathematics for Computer Science
Credit Hours: 3
Prerequisites: CS 1301 (grade of C or better) or CS 1315 (grade of C or better)
Offered: Fall, Spring

Introduction to mathematical foundations required for computer science. Topics:
logic and proofs, sets, relations, functions, induction and recursion, counting
and combinatorics, graph theory, number theory, and introductory probability.
Prepares students for CS 3510 (Design and Analysis of Algorithms).
Note: CS 2051 is the Honors version of this course; credit not awarded for both.
Minimum grade to satisfy prerequisite: C or better.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

CS 2110 — Computer Organization and Programming
Credit Hours: 4
Prerequisites: CS 1331 (grade of C or better)
Offered: Fall, Spring

An introduction to basic computer organization: datapaths, the memory hierarchy,
assembly language (LC-3), C programming, and system-level programming. Students
gain understanding of how high-level programs map to machine instructions, how
memory is managed, and how hardware affects software performance. Core requirement
for Systems and Architecture thread.
Minimum grade to satisfy prerequisite: C or better.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

CS 2200 — Systems and Networks
Credit Hours: 4
Prerequisites: CS 2110 (grade of C or better)
Offered: Fall, Spring

Provides a broad view of systems: operating system structure, process management,
concurrency, synchronization, memory management, file systems, I/O, networking
fundamentals, and distributed systems. Combines both systems programming (in C)
and theoretical understanding.
Minimum grade to satisfy prerequisite: C or better.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

CS 2340 — Objects and Design
Credit Hours: 3
Prerequisites: CS 1332 (grade of C or better)
Offered: Fall, Spring

Object-oriented programming methods for dealing with large programs. Focus on
quality processes, effective debugging techniques, and testing to assure a quality
product. Topics: design patterns, UML, agile development, version control,
refactoring, unit testing, and software architecture. Team project required.
Minimum grade to satisfy prerequisite: C or better.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

CS 2316 — Data Manipulation and Analysis
Credit Hours: 3
Prerequisites: CS 1331 (grade of C or better)
Offered: Fall, Spring

Input, storage, manipulation, and presentation of data using Python. Topics include
web scraping, REST APIs, CSV/JSON/XML processing, SQL databases, data cleaning with
pandas, and data visualization with matplotlib. Bridges programming fundamentals and
data science.
Minimum grade to satisfy prerequisite: C or better.

CS 3510 — Design and Analysis of Algorithms
Credit Hours: 3
Prerequisites: CS 1332 (grade of C or better) AND CS 2050 (grade of C or better)
Offered: Fall, Spring

Basic techniques of design and analysis of efficient algorithms for standard
computational problems and NP-Completeness. Topics include divide-and-conquer,
dynamic programming, greedy algorithms, network flow, randomized algorithms,
and reductions between problems. Mathematical proof of correctness required.
Note: Credit not allowed for both CS 3510 and CS 3511 (Honors version).
Minimum grade to satisfy prerequisite: C or better.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

CS 3600 — Introduction to Artificial Intelligence
Credit Hours: 3
Prerequisites: CS 1332 (grade of C or better)
Offered: Fall, Spring

An introduction to artificial intelligence and machine learning. Topics include
intelligent system design methodologies, search and problem solving (BFS, DFS,
A*, minimax), supervised learning (decision trees, neural networks basics),
unsupervised learning (k-means), and reinforcement learning fundamentals.
Minimum grade to satisfy prerequisite: C or better.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

CS 3630 — Introduction to Perception and Robotics
Credit Hours: 3
Prerequisites: CS 1332 (grade of C or better)
Offered: Fall, Spring

Covers fundamental problems and leading solutions for computer and robot perception
and action from the point of view of autonomous robot navigation. Topics: sensors
and actuators, probabilistic state estimation, localization, path planning, computer
vision basics.
Minimum grade to satisfy prerequisite: C or better.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

CS 4400 — Introduction to Database Systems
Credit Hours: 3
Prerequisites: CS 1301 (grade of C or better) AND CS 2340 (grade of C or better)
  OR instructor consent
Offered: Fall, Spring

Relational database design and implementation. Topics: entity-relationship modeling,
relational algebra, SQL (DDL, DML, DCL), functional dependencies, normalization
(1NF through BCNF), transaction management, concurrency control, and introduction
to NoSQL. Students design and build a complete database-backed application.
Minimum grade to satisfy prerequisite: C or better.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

CS 4510 — Automata and Complexity
Credit Hours: 3
Prerequisites: CS 2050 (grade of C or better) AND CS 3510 (grade of C or better)
Offered: Fall, Spring

Theoretical models of computation. Topics: finite automata, regular languages,
context-free languages and grammars, pushdown automata, Turing machines, decidability,
the halting problem, and complexity classes (P, NP, NP-Completeness, PSPACE).
Minimum grade to satisfy prerequisite: C or better.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

CS 4641 — Machine Learning
Credit Hours: 3
Prerequisites: CS 3600 (grade of C or better) AND MATH 2603 or MATH 3012
  (grade of C or better)
Offered: Fall, Spring

Supervised and unsupervised machine learning. Topics: regression, classification
(logistic regression, SVM, neural networks, decision trees, ensembles), clustering
(k-means, EM), dimensionality reduction (PCA), model evaluation and validation,
bias-variance tradeoff, and feature engineering. Significant programming component
in Python using scikit-learn and PyTorch.
Minimum grade to satisfy prerequisite: C or better.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

CS 4650 — Natural Language Processing
Credit Hours: 3
Prerequisites: CS 3600 (grade of C or better)
Offered: Spring

Statistical and neural approaches to NLP. Topics: language modeling (n-gram,
neural), text classification, sequence models (RNN, LSTM, transformers), named
entity recognition, parsing, machine translation, question answering, and
fine-tuning large language models. Programming assignments in Python/PyTorch.
Minimum grade to satisfy prerequisite: C or better.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

CS 4731 — Game AI
Credit Hours: 3
Prerequisites: CS 3600 (grade of C or better)
Offered: Fall

Application of artificial intelligence techniques to games. Topics: pathfinding,
decision making, strategy, and learning in games. Project-based course.
Minimum grade to satisfy prerequisite: C or better.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

CS 4803 — Special Topics in CS (Deep Learning)
Credit Hours: 3
Prerequisites: CS 4641 (grade of C or better)
Offered: Fall, Spring (varies by topic)

Advanced deep learning: convolutional networks, recurrent networks, transformers,
generative models (VAE, GAN, Diffusion), and reinforcement learning. Current
research papers assigned. Significant project component. Instructor consent
may be required for overrides.
Minimum grade to satisfy prerequisite: C or better.

SECTION 3: MATHEMATICS REQUIREMENTS FOR CS
================================================================================

MATH 1551 — Differential Calculus
Credit Hours: 2
Prerequisites: MATH 1113 (Precalculus, grade of C or better) or placement exam
Offered: Fall, Spring, Summer

Differential calculus: limits, continuity, derivatives, differentiation rules,
applications of derivatives (optimization, related rates, curve sketching), and
introduction to integration.
Minimum grade to satisfy prerequisite: C or better.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

MATH 1552 — Integral Calculus
Credit Hours: 4
Prerequisites: MATH 1551 (grade of C or better)
Offered: Fall, Spring, Summer

Integral calculus: antiderivatives, definite integrals, fundamental theorem of
calculus, techniques of integration, improper integrals, sequences and series,
and introduction to differential equations.
Minimum grade to satisfy prerequisite: C or better.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

MATH 1554 — Linear Algebra
Credit Hours: 4
Prerequisites: MATH 1551 (grade of C or better) or concurrent enrollment
Offered: Fall, Spring, Summer

Systems of linear equations, matrices, determinants, vector spaces, linear
transformations, eigenvalues and eigenvectors, orthogonality, and applications
to computer science and engineering. Required for CS 4641 (Machine Learning).
Minimum grade to satisfy prerequisite: C or better.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

MATH 2603 — Introduction to Discrete Mathematics
Credit Hours: 3
Prerequisites: MATH 1551 (grade of C or better)
Offered: Fall, Spring

Discrete structures for computer science: logic, sets, relations, functions,
induction, combinatorics, graph theory, and number theory. Alternative to CS 2050
for students in certain tracks.
Minimum grade to satisfy prerequisite: C or better.

SECTION 4: B.S. COMPUTER SCIENCE — DEGREE REQUIREMENTS (2024-2025)
================================================================================

Source: https://catalog.gatech.edu/programs/computer-science-bs/
Total credit hours required: 120

--- Required Core Courses (All students must complete) ---

  CS 1301  — Introduction to Computing (3 cr)
  CS 1331  — Introduction to OOP (3 cr)
  CS 1332  — Data Structures and Algorithms (3 cr)
  CS 2050  — Intro to Discrete Mathematics (3 cr)
  CS 2110  — Computer Organization and Programming (4 cr)
  CS 2200  — Systems and Networks (4 cr)
  CS 2340  — Objects and Design (3 cr)
  CS 3510  — Design and Analysis of Algorithms (3 cr)
  CS 3600  — Introduction to Artificial Intelligence (3 cr)
  CS 4400  — Introduction to Database Systems (3 cr)
  CS 4510  — Automata and Complexity (3 cr)

All required CS courses must be completed with a grade of C or better
to count toward degree requirements. Pass/fail grading is NOT permitted
for required CS courses (only allowed for free electives, max 6 hours,
and CS 1100 Freshman Leap Seminar).

--- Thread Requirements ---

Each student must select TWO Threads from the following nine options:
  1. Devices
  2. Information Internetworks
  3. Intelligence
  4. Media
  5. Modeling and Simulation
  6. People
  7. Systems and Architecture
  8. Theory
  9. Cybersecurity and Privacy

Each Thread requires approximately 15-18 additional credit hours of
Thread-specific elective courses.

--- Mathematics and Science Requirements ---

  MATH 1551 — Differential Calculus (2 cr)
  MATH 1552 — Integral Calculus (4 cr)
  MATH 1554 — Linear Algebra (4 cr)
  MATH 2603 or CS 2050 — Discrete Mathematics (3 cr)
  Lab Sciences: Minimum two lab science courses, at least one sequence
    (e.g., PHYS 2211 + PHYS 2212, or CHEM 1310 + CHEM 1315, etc.)

--- Humanities, Social Sciences, and Languages (HSOC) ---

  Minimum 18 credit hours of Humanities/Social Science courses.
  Must include: Ethics requirement (CS 3001 Computing and Society, 3 cr)
  No more than 6 credit hours from any single department.

--- Free Electives ---

  12 credit hours of free electives.
  Up to 6 of these hours may be taken on a pass/fail basis.
  No 1000- or 2000-level MATH (except approved sequences) may count.

--- Residency Requirement ---

  At least 36 credit hours must be completed in residence at Georgia Tech
  (as opposed to transfer credit). This includes the final 30 credit hours
  of the degree.

--- GPA Requirement ---

  Minimum cumulative GPA of 2.0 required for graduation.
  No single required CS course may be completed with below a C.

--- Prerequisite Policy ---

A prerequisite is a course that must be completed (passed with C or better)
BEFORE enrolling in the subsequent course. Prerequisites are enforced at
registration. A student found to have enrolled without meeting prerequisites
may be administratively withdrawn.

A co-requisite is a course that may be completed concurrently (in the same
semester) as the course it supports. Co-requisites must be enrolled in the
same term unless the student has already completed the co-requisite course.

Exception: A student may request a prerequisite override from the course
instructor. Instructor consent overrides require written approval and are
granted at the discretion of the faculty member. Consent of instructor
does NOT guarantee enrollment if the course is full.

--- Minimum Grade Policy ---

A grade of C (2.0) or better is required for any course to satisfy a
prerequisite requirement. Grades of C- (1.7), D (1.0), or D- (0.7) do
NOT satisfy prerequisite requirements, even though they are passing grades
for GPA purposes. A grade of F, W, WF, or I does not satisfy any prerequisite.

--- Course Repeat Policy ---

Students may repeat a course if they receive a grade of C- or lower (including F).
A student who has earned a C or higher may NOT repeat the course for credit
without special dean approval.

When a course is repeated at Georgia Tech:
  - Both grades appear on the official transcript.
  - The HIGHER grade replaces the lower grade in GPA computation
    (grade replacement / grade forgiveness policy).
  - A course may be repeated at most twice without Dean of Students approval.
  - The course credit hours count only ONCE toward total degree hours.

--- Credit Hour Load Policy ---

Standard full-time enrollment: 12–18 credit hours per semester.
Enrollment above 18 credit hours requires academic advisor approval.
Part-time status (fewer than 12 hours) may affect financial aid eligibility.
Summer enrollment is limited to 14 credit hours without special approval.

--- Transfer Credit Policy ---

Transfer coursework from accredited institutions is evaluated by the
Office of the Registrar. Equivalent courses may satisfy degree requirements
and prerequisites pending formal Registrar evaluation.
  - Maximum 60 transfer credit hours may be applied toward the 120-hour degree.
  - Transfer students must complete at least 36 credit hours at Georgia Tech.
  - Official transcripts required. Evaluation may take 4-6 weeks.
  - Students may NOT enroll in courses for which transfer equivalency has not
    yet been confirmed by the Registrar.

--- Academic Integrity Policy ---

All submitted work must be the student's own. Collaboration is permitted only
as explicitly stated in the course syllabus. Unauthorized collaboration,
plagiarism, or use of AI-generated content without disclosure constitutes
academic misconduct. Violations are subject to the Georgia Tech Honor Code and
may result in a 0 on the assignment, a failing grade in the course, or
academic dismissal.
